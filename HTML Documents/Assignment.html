Microbiology

Bacteria and viruses are cool!

Let's learn about them.

Bacteria are prokaryotic organisms that are ubiquitous within the environment and our bodies.

Viruses are biological entities that are able to infect a host and are usually made of genetic material in a protien coat.

When we look closely at the microscopic world around us there are many agents that can cause disease. It is important to know how they work and how to categorize them so that we may better understand them and apply this knowledge to the field of medicine.

Here is a quote from Encyclopedia Britannica:

The field is concerned with the structure, function, and classification of such organisms and with ways of both exploiting and controlling their activities.
